07 Research Directions

1. Synthetic Gene Circuits & Cellular Programming

Engineering multi-layered genetic circuits that can perform complex logic operations inside living cells. Recent work has focused on building circuits robust enough to function in mammalian therapeutic cells, with applications in cancer immunotherapy and autoimmune disease. This is the lab's flagship research area and the most funded direction.

2. Computational Modeling of Gene Regulatory Networks

Developing physics-informed machine learning models to predict gene expression dynamics from promoter sequence and chromatin state. The lab combines experimental perturbation data from CRISPR screens with deep learning to build predictive models of transcriptional regulation. This area is highly collaborative and intersects with several labs at MIT and the Broad Institute.

Building next-generation pooled CRISPR screening tools with single-cell readouts (Perturb-seq).
