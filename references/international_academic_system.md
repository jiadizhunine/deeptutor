# International Academic System Reference

Quick reference for evaluating professors and labs outside of China.
Covers academic ranks, funding, prestige indicators, tenure, university tiers, student types, and useful databases.

---

## 1. Academic Ranks

### United States

| Rank | Typical Timeline | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| Assistant Professor (tenure-track) | Years 0-6 | Probationary period, must earn tenure |
| Associate Professor (tenured) | After ~6 years | Tenure granted, permanent position |
| Full Professor | Varies, 5-15 years post-tenure | Senior rank, often department leadership |
| Endowed Chair / Distinguished Professor | By reputation | Named position with dedicated funding |
| University Professor | Rare, highest honor | Institution-wide appointment, e.g. Harvard "University Professor" |

Non-tenure-track: Research Professor, Clinical Professor, Teaching Professor, Adjunct -- these carry no tenure and often less stability.

### United Kingdom

| Rank | US Equivalent (rough) | Notes |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| Lecturer | Assistant Professor | Entry-level permanent academic post |
| Senior Lecturer | Associate Professor | Mid-career, may supervise PhD students |
| Reader | Between Assoc. and Full Prof. | Strong research record, pre-professorial |
| Professor | Full Professor | Selective, typically <20% of faculty |
| Regius Professor / Named Chair | Distinguished / Endowed Chair | Highest honor, often historically significant |

UK posts are typically permanent from Lecturer onward (no tenure clock), but promotion is competitive.

### European Union (varies by country)

| Country | Junior Rank | Senior Rank | Notes |
|---------|------------|-------------|-------|
| Germany | Juniorprofessor (W1) / Habilitation | Professor (W2/W3) | W3 is full chair, very competitive |
| France | Maitre de conferences (MCF) | Professeur des universites | MCF is permanent, promotion requires national qualification |
| Netherlands | Universitair Docent (UD) | Universitair Hoofddocent (UHD) / Hoogleraar | Hoogleraar = full professor |
| Switzerland | Assistant Professor (tenure-track) | Associate / Full Professor | ETH/EPFL follow US-style tenure system |
| Scandinavia | Assistant Professor / Researcher | Associate Professor / Professor | Varies; some have tenure-track, some do not |

## 2. Major Grants & Funding

### United States

| Grant | Agency | Amount | Significance |
|-------|--------|--------|-------------|
| R01 | NIH | $250K-500K/yr for 3-5 yrs | Bread and butter of biomedical research; having one = established PI |
| R21 | NIH | ~$275K over 2 yrs | Exploratory/developmental; smaller, good for new directions |
| K99/R00 | NIH | K99 (postdoc) + R00 (faculty) | "Kangaroo award" -- postdoc-to-PI transition, very competitive |
| DP2 | NIH | ~$1.5M over 5 yrs | Director's New Innovator Award; early-career, high-risk/high-reward |
| CAREER | NSF | $500K-800K over 5 yrs | Premier early-career award; almost required for tenure at top schools |
| Standard Grant | NSF | $150K-500K, varies | Bread and butter of non-biomedical science |
| DOE Early Career | DOE | $750K over 5 yrs | Important for physical sciences, energy research |
| Young Investigator (YIP) | DOD/AFOSR/ONR/ARO | $150K-500K over 3 yrs | Military research agencies, early-career |
| DARPA | DARPA | Varies widely | High-risk, high-impact; often large multi-PI projects |

**Rule of thumb (US biomed):** 1 R01 = competent PI. 2+ R01s = well-funded lab. R01 + DP2/HHMI = star.

### European Union

| Grant | Agency | Amount | Significance |
|-------|--------|--------|-------------|
| ERC Starting Grant | ERC | up to 1.5M EUR / 5 yrs | For PIs 2-7 years post-PhD; gold standard for EU early career |
| ERC Consolidator Grant | ERC | up to 2M EUR / 5 yrs | For PIs 7-12 years post-PhD; confirms independent leadership |
| ERC Advanced Grant | ERC | up to 2.5M EUR / 5 yrs | For established leaders; EU equivalent of being a "big name" |
| ERC Synergy Grant | ERC | up to 10M EUR / 6 yrs | 2-4 PIs jointly; large collaborative |
| Marie Sklodowska-Curie Fellowship | EU | Varies | Postdoctoral mobility fellowship; well-regarded |
| Horizon Europe Collaborative | EU | Varies, often multi-million | Large consortium grants; good funding but less prestige per PI |

**Rule of thumb (EU):** ERC grant = top-tier. Starting Grant winner at a young age is an exceptionally strong signal.

### United Kingdom

## 3. Key Prestige Indicators

From strongest to more common, roughly:

| Indicator | What It Means |
|-----------|--------------|
| Nobel Prize / Fields Medal / Turing Award | Pinnacle of scientific recognition |
| National Academy of Sciences (NAS) member | Top ~0.1% of US scientists |
| HHMI Investigator | Top-tier US biologist, guaranteed long-term funding |
| ERC Advanced Grant holder | Leading European researcher |
| National Academy of Engineering / Medicine | Domain-specific elite recognition |
| Endowed / Named Chair | University's investment in retaining a star |
| ERC Starting/Consolidator Grant | Strong early/mid-career European PI |
| NIH R01 (multiple) | Well-funded, productive US biomedical lab |
| NSF CAREER Award | Early-career milestone, expected at top US departments |
| Sloan Research Fellow | Competitive early-career (US/Canada), 2-year term |
| Packard / Searle / Pew Scholar | Early-career fellowships, strong signal in respective fields |
| Fellow of professional society (e.g., IEEE Fellow, APS Fellow) | Senior recognition within a discipline |

**For students evaluating a PI:** Focus on active grants (not expired ones), h-index trajectory, and whether students are publishing first-author papers in good venues.

## 4. The Tenure System (for students unfamiliar)

### What Is Tenure?

Tenure is a permanent employment guarantee at a university. Once tenured, a professor can only be dismissed for extraordinary cause (e.g., misconduct). It exists primarily in the US and some institutions that follow the US model.

### The 6-Year Clock (US)

1. **Year 0:** Hired as Assistant Professor (tenure-track).
2. **Years 1-5:** Build lab, publish, get grants, train students, teach. Annual reviews.
3. **Year 6:** Tenure decision by committee. External letters solicited. Up-or-out: either promoted to Associate Professor (tenured) or contract ends.
4. **Post-tenure:** Associate Professor, then eventually Full Professor by further promotion.

### How This Affects Students

| Factor | Pre-tenure PI | Post-tenure PI |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Work intensity | Higher -- PI is fighting for career survival | Variable -- depends on personality |
| Funding pressure | Intense -- needs grants to survive review | More stable, but still needs grants |
| Student publication pressure | High -- PI needs papers for tenure case | Lower urgency, but still important |
| Mentorship time | May be less available (writing grants, teaching) | Often more available |
| Lab size | Usually smaller, more direct mentorship | Often larger, more hierarchy |
| Risk for students | If PI doesn't get tenure, lab closes | Stable |
| Opportunity | Ground floor of a rising lab; closer relationship | Established network and reputation |

**Practical advice:** A pre-tenure PI at a strong institution who has secured initial funding (e.g., CAREER + R01) is often an excellent choice -- motivated, accessible, and building their name. But verify they have funding and that their institution is supportive.

## 6. Student & Researcher Types

| Position | Duration | Key Features |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| PhD Student (US) | 5-7 years | Coursework + qualifying exam + dissertation; funded by RA/TA |
| PhD Student (UK) | 3-4 years | Research-focused from day one; less coursework |
| PhD Student (EU) | 3-4 years | Often hired as employee (salary, benefits); structured programs |
| Postdoctoral Researcher | 2-5 years | Transitional; building independent publication record |
| Research Associate / Research Scientist | Ongoing | Permanent-ish staff scientist; no tenure track |
| Visiting Scholar / Visiting Professor | 3 months - 1 year | Temporary affiliation, often for sabbatical or collaboration |
| Research Assistant (RA) | 1-3 years | Pre-PhD staff position, common in US labs |

**For students evaluating a lab:** Check where the PI's former PhD students and postdocs ended up. This is the single strongest signal of mentorship quality.
